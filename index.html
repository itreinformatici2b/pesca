Fishing Industry

The commercial fishing industry is a complex global network that provides food, employment, and economic stability to coastal communities worldwide.

Global Impact

Over 200 million people depend on fishing for their livelihoods, with annual catches exceeding 90 million metric tons worldwide.

$362B Global Market Value

Commercial fishing targets over 500 species, from small pelagic fish to large deep-sea creatures, each requiring specialized techniques.

Sustainable Practices

The industry is evolving toward more sustainable methods, including quota systems, selective fishing gear, and marine protected areas to ensure long-term viability.

  • Certification programs (MSC, ASC)
  • Bycatch reduction technologies
  • Ecosystem-based management

Fishing Tools & Technology

Modern commercial fishing relies on sophisticated tools and technology to locate, catch, and process fish efficiently and sustainably.

Sonar Systems

Advanced sonar technology detects fish schools up to 2km away, providing real-time 3D imaging.

GPS Navigation

Precise satellite navigation allows vessels to return to productive fishing grounds accurately.

Echo Sounders

Measures water depth and detects fish by sending sound pulses and analyzing their echoes.

Ocean Sensors

Monitors water temperature, salinity, and chlorophyll levels to identify optimal fishing conditions.

Hooks, Lines & Traps

Selective fishing methods like hooks and traps target specific species while minimizing bycatch and environmental impact compared to non-selective methods.

Longlining

Main line with thousands of baited hooks deployed over miles, primarily targeting tuna, swordfish, and halibut.

Selectivity: High Bycatch: Medium

Pots & Traps

Baited cages or pots that capture lobsters, crabs, and fish alive, allowing undersized animals to be released unharmed.

Selectivity: Very High Bycatch: Low

Handlines & Poles

Simple single-hook methods used by artisanal fishers, offering maximum selectivity and minimal habitat damage.

Selectivity: Highest Bycatch: Very Low

Innovation in Fishing Gear

Recent innovations include circle hooks that reduce turtle bycatch, escape panels in traps, and LED lights that attract target species while repelling others.

Circle hooks reduce sea turtle mortality by 90%
Escape panels allow undersized crabs to exit traps
LED lights increase target catch while reducing bycatch
